The XML Sitemap Guide for AI and Search

A clean sitemap helps every crawler — AI included — find and prioritize your pages. Here's how to build and submit one.

By GeoPageScan·Jul 9, 2026·4 min read

What a sitemap does

A sitemap lists your important URLs so crawlers discover them quickly and understand which pages matter, with optional lastmod and priority hints.

Build a good one

Include canonical, indexable URLs only; keep it current with lastmod dates; and reference it from robots.txt so AI crawlers find it automatically.

Submit and monitor

Submit your sitemap in Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools, and keep it updated as you publish — your blog generator can rebuild it automatically.

Frequently asked questions

Do AI crawlers use sitemaps?⌄
Yes — a sitemap referenced in robots.txt helps AI and search crawlers discover and prioritize your pages faster.
What belongs in a sitemap?⌄
Only canonical, indexable URLs, ideally with lastmod dates. Exclude redirects, duplicates and noindex pages.
Where do I submit my sitemap?⌄
Reference it in robots.txt and submit it in Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools.
